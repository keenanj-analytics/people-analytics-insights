# 5. Engagement

**Question:** Which teams are engaged and which are disengaging? What themes are driving the gap?

---

## Key Findings

**Sales has the lowest engagement score in the organization, and the three weakest themes org-wide -- Recognition, Career Growth, and Communication -- map directly to the top voluntary attrition reasons.** The 2025 H2 engagement survey shows an org-wide average score of 3.65 out of 5.00, with a favorable response rate of 58%. Sales sits at the bottom at 3.49, followed by G&A (3.63) and Executive (3.64). The largest cycle-over-cycle declines are concentrated in Executive, where Employee Engagement dropped 0.63 points -- the steepest single-theme decline in the organization.

---

## Org-Wide Engagement by Theme

The 2025 H2 survey measured 8 themes. Ranked from lowest to highest average score:

| Theme | Avg Score | Favorable % |
|-------|----------|------------|
| Recognition | 3.53 | 52% |
| Career Growth & Development | 3.56 | 54% |
| Communication & Transparency | 3.57 | 55% |
| Resources & Enablement | 3.65 | 57% |
| Work-Life Balance | 3.65 | 57% |
| Culture & Values | 3.74 | 62% |
| Manager Effectiveness | 3.74 | 61% |
| Employee Engagement | 3.83 | 66% |

The bottom three themes -- Recognition (3.53), Career Growth (3.56), and Communication (3.57) -- deserve attention because they align with why people are leaving. From [Section 2](02_attrition.md): Career Opportunities is the #1 voluntary termination reason (36%), and Compensation is #3 (15%). Low scores in Career Growth and Recognition suggest employees feel their contributions are not being recognized and their development paths are not clear. These are the conditions that make a recruiter's call compelling.

A favorable rate below 55% means that nearly half of respondents did not agree with positive statements about recognition or career growth. In engagement survey methodology, scores below 60% favorable are typically flagged as areas requiring intervention.

### Sales: Lowest Engagement, Highest Manager-Related Attrition

Sales is a clear outlier at 3.49, sitting 0.16 points below the org average. This is the same department where Manager Relationship is the #1 attrition reason at 26% (vs. 10% org-wide). The engagement data reinforces the attrition signal: Sales employees are less engaged than their peers across the organization, and when they leave, a disproportionate number cite their manager as the reason.

### Customer Success: High Engagement, High Attrition -- A Paradox

Customer Success scores 3.74, the second-highest in the organization. Yet it has the highest annualized attrition rate (42%). This is not contradictory -- it suggests CS employees are engaged with their *work* but leaving for *external* reasons (career opportunities, compensation). They are not disengaged; they are undercompensated and underleveraged. Engaged employees who leave are the most regrettable losses because they were productive right up to the point of departure.

### Executive: Steepest Declines

Executive's overall score of 3.64 is near the org average, but the cycle-over-cycle trends are alarming:

| Theme | H2 Score | H1 to H2 Change |
|-------|---------|-----------------|
| Employee Engagement | 3.47 | -0.63 |
| Communication & Transparency | 3.63 | -0.30 |
| Manager Effectiveness | 3.70 | -0.25 |
| Culture & Values | 3.70 | -0.17 |

A 0.63-point drop in Employee Engagement in a single cycle is a red flag at any level, but at the Executive level it signals potential leadership instability. Combined with 100% regrettable attrition in Executive (4 of 4 departures) and 75% top performer loss, this suggests the senior leadership team is experiencing a confidence or direction crisis that the survey is capturing.

## Recommended Actions

1. **Launch a Sales-specific engagement action plan.** Sales is the only department that is both the lowest-engaged and has a unique attrition driver (Manager Relationship). Focus on manager effectiveness within Sales -- 360 feedback, manager coaching, and potentially structural changes to reporting lines in Account Management where attrition is highest.

2. **Address Recognition and Career Growth org-wide.** These are the two lowest-scoring themes and both map to the top attrition reason. Tactical interventions: implement a structured recognition program (peer-to-peer, manager-to-IC), publish career progression frameworks for high-attrition roles (CSM, SDR, Engineer), and increase frequency of career development conversations from annual to quarterly.

3. **Investigate Executive engagement decline immediately.** The 0.63-point drop in Employee Engagement at the executive level is too large to wait for the next survey cycle. Conduct 1:1 conversations with senior leaders to understand what is driving the decline before it cascades into broader organizational instability.

4. **Do not over-index on Customer Success engagement scores.** CS scores well on engagement but has the highest attrition rate. The intervention for CS is compensation and career pathing ([Sections 2](02_attrition.md) and [4](04_compensation.md)), not engagement programming.
