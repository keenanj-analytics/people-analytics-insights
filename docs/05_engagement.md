# 5. Engagement

**Question:** Which teams are engaged and which are disengaging? What themes are driving the gap?

---

## Summary of Findings

1. Org-wide eNPS has held steady in the 55-57 range since 2022, currently at 57. Engagement survived the 2024 RIF without a major dip but has not improved since.
2. Department-level eNPS varies widely -- from G&A (40) to Product (72). Customer Success has the second-highest eNPS (71) despite having the highest attrition rate (42%), indicating employees are engaged but leaving for external reasons.
3. Sales has the lowest engagement scores across nearly every theme (3.4-3.6 range), consistent with the manager-driven attrition signal. G&A has the lowest eNPS (40) despite low attrition and the highest compa-ratio.

---

## 1 - Org-wide eNPS has held steady at 55-57 since 2022, but has not recovered to pre-scale levels

![Companywide eNPS Over Time](../dashboards/E1_eNPS_Orgwide_OverTime.png)

The org-wide eNPS dropped from 66 in 2021 H1 to 52 in 2021 H2 -- likely reflecting the rapid scaling pressures of hypergrowth -- then stabilized in the 55-57 range for the past four years. The current score of 57 is healthy by industry standards (above 50 is generally considered "good"), but the flat trajectory means engagement has not improved despite the company's recovery and growth since the 2024 RIF.

---

## 2 - Department eNPS varies widely, with Customer Success paradoxically high (71) and Sales consistently low (46)

![eNPS by Department (2025 H2)](../dashboards/E2_eNPS_by_Department.png)

The department spread is 32 points from G&A (40) to Product (72). Three stories stand out:

**Customer Success at 71 is a paradox.** Second-highest eNPS in the organization, yet the highest annualized attrition rate (42%) and the lowest offer acceptance rate. CS employees are not disengaged -- they are undercompensated and underleveraged. Engaged employees who leave are the most regrettable losses because they were productive right up to the point of departure.

**Sales at 46 is consistent with the attrition signal.** Manager Relationship is the #1 attrition reason in Sales at 26% (vs. 10% org-wide). The low eNPS confirms this is showing up in how current employees feel, not just in exit interviews.

**G&A at 40 is the lowest despite low attrition and the highest compa-ratio (1.01).** G&A employees are paid well and staying, but they are not enthusiastic. This may reflect role satisfaction or growth ceiling concerns rather than an immediate retention risk.

---

## 3 - Sales scores lowest across nearly every theme; Career Growth and Recognition are the weakest themes org-wide

![Avg Theme Score by Department (2025 H2)](../dashboards/E3_Avg_Theme_Score_by_Department.png)

Sales scores lowest across nearly every theme (3.4-3.6 range), with Career Growth & Development (3.4) and Communication & Transparency (3.4) as the bottom two. These align directly with the attrition drivers: when employees do not see a career path and do not feel informed, a recruiter's call becomes compelling.

The bottom three themes org-wide -- Recognition, Career Growth & Development, and Communication & Transparency -- all score in the 3.5-3.6 range. These map to the top voluntary termination reasons from [Section 2](02_attrition.md): Career Opportunities (#1 at 36%) and Compensation (#3 at 15%). Together, low Career Growth and low Recognition create the conditions that make external offers attractive.
