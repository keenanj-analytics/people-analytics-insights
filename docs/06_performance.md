# 6. Performance

**Question:** Is our rating distribution healthy or inflated? Where is top talent concentrated?

---

## Summary of Findings

1. JustKaizen's performance rating distribution is healthy and well-calibrated, with 55% of active employees rated "Meets Expectations" and a clean bell-curve shape. The distribution (25% top, 55% middle, 20% bottom) falls within industry benchmarks.
2. 350 active employees (31%) are classified as top performers. Engineering has the largest absolute pool (n=126), followed by Sales (n=58). Customer Success has the lowest top performer rate at 22%.
3. Average performance rating by department is tightly clustered (2.90-3.19), suggesting reasonable cross-department calibration consistency.

---

## 1 - Rating distribution is healthy with 55% at "Meets Expectations" -- managers are differentiating performance, not inflating

![2025 Year End Performance Rating Distribution](../dashboards/P1_Performance_Distribution_Overall.png)

*Distribution reflects active employees only (n = 1,144).*

The 55% concentration at "Meets Expectations" indicates that managers are not inflating ratings -- a common problem in tech companies where 70%+ of employees often receive the top two ratings. The 20% rated below "Meets" (Partially Meets + Does Not Meet) suggests the calibration process is functioning. Industry benchmarks from Mercer and WorldatWork suggest a healthy distribution has 50-60% in the middle tier, 20-30% in the top two, and 10-20% in the bottom two. JustKaizen's distribution falls within these ranges.

---

## 2 - 31% of active employees are top performers, concentrated in Engineering (n=126) and Sales (n=58)

![Top Performers - Org Wide](../dashboards/P2_Top_Performers_Overall.png)

350 active employees (31%) are classified as top performers -- high enough to represent meaningful differentiation, but not so high that the label loses its meaning.

![Top Performers by Department](../dashboards/P3_Top_Performers_Department.png)

Engineering has the largest absolute pool of top performers (n=126), followed by Sales (n=58). The concentration matters because Engineering lost 9 top performers and Sales lost 4 in Q1 2026 alone. At that rate, Engineering would lose 36 top performers annually -- nearly 29% of its top talent pool.

Customer Success has the lowest top performer rate at 22%. This could reflect genuine performance distribution differences, or it could indicate that calibration standards in CS are more conservative. CS also has the lowest average rating (2.90), which may warrant a calibration review.

---

## 3 - Average rating by department is tightly clustered (2.90-3.19), suggesting reasonable cross-department consistency

![Avg Performance Rating by Department](../dashboards/P4_Avg_Performance_rating_dept.png)

The spread from top (People: 3.19) to bottom (Customer Success: 2.90) is 0.29 points -- a narrow range. Customer Success and Executive sitting at the bottom may reflect the structural disruption these departments have experienced (CS headcount decline, Executive turnover at 133% annualized) rather than genuinely lower performance.
